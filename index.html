Capture Protocol Reference

Industry-standard capture patterns for SfM + segmentation pipelines. All protocols assume conference-room furniture (central table, chairs around perimeter) — walking happens in the corridor between furniture and walls. Tap a station to see its details.

v0.2 · 2026-05-12 · furniture-aware
4-Corner Protocol

Best for small to mid-sized rectangular rooms (≤10m on the long side). Each station sweeps 90° from one adjacent wall to the other. Walking between corners provides the translation baseline for SfM.

Key principle

Every wall is seen from at least two corners, giving SfM strong angular diversity and ~5m+ baseline between viewpoints — much better than typical photogrammetry overlap.

~5–6m huddle room · clockwise perimeter walk · corners accessible
Capture station
90° fan
Walking path
Step-by-step
  • 1
    Corner 1 — face Wall A
    Stand in the corner where walls D and A meet. Point your camera along Wall A (you're looking down the length of A toward corner 2). Tap Begin, then sweep right 90° until you're looking down Wall D.
    90° fan · ~6s sweep
  • 2
    Corner 2 — face Wall B
    Walk along Wall A to the next corner (A meets B). Point camera along Wall B. Sweep right 90° to face Wall A.
    90° fan · ~6s sweep
  • 3
    Corner 3 — face Wall C
    Walk along Wall B to the next corner (B meets C). Point camera along Wall C. Sweep right 90° to face Wall B.
    90° fan · ~6s sweep
  • 4
    Corner 4 — face Wall D
    Walk to the final corner. Point camera along Wall D. Sweep right 90° to face Wall C. Capture complete.
    90° fan · ~6s sweep
Tips for testing
  • Always walk clockwise — keeps left-to-right sweep direction consistent with your motion
  • Hold camera at chest height; tilt slightly down to capture floor edges
  • The "face Wall A" instruction means "look parallel to that wall surface" — your view is along its length
  • Each wall ends up captured from two corners with strong angular diversity → solid SfM input
Perimeter Loop · 8-Station

For long boardrooms or classrooms with a central conference table. Walk the corridor between the table and the walls; stop at corners and along long walls to capture the opposite wall from close range. No center walk required.

Key principle

Every wall must be captured from within ~5m. In a long room, you can't reach that distance to opposite long walls from the corners alone, so insert additional stations along each long wall facing across the table.

~15m × 5m boardroom · clockwise corridor walk · 8 stations on perimeter
Station
90° fan
Table / chairs
Walking corridor
Step-by-step (15m room — scale up for longer)
  • 1
    NW corner
    Start in the corner where Short A meets Long N. Face along Long N (look down the length of N toward NE corner). Sweep right 90° until you face along Short A. Captures both adjacent walls at the corner.
    90° fan · corner station
  • 2
    Long N · 1/3 along
    Walk ~5m east along Long N, staying in the corridor between the wall and the chairs. Turn to face Long S directly (perpendicular to your walk). Sweep right 90° — start looking SW (back toward the SW area), end looking SE (toward the next station's region). Captures a section of Long S from close range.
    90° fan · faces across the table
  • 3
    Long N · 2/3 along
    Continue east along Long N. Face Long S again. Sweep right 90° (same procedure as station 2). This second N-wall midpoint covers the eastern half of Long S that station 2 didn't reach.
    90° fan · faces across the table
  • 4
    NE corner
    Continue to the NE corner. Face along Short B. Sweep right 90° until you face along Long N (looking back west). Captures both walls at the NE corner.
    90° fan · corner station
  • 5
    SE corner
    Walk south along Short B to the SE corner. Face along Long S (looking west). Sweep right 90° to face along Short B.
    90° fan · corner station
  • 6
    Long S · 2/3 along
    Walk ~5m west along Long S, again in the corridor. Face Long N (perpendicular). Sweep right 90° — same procedure as stations 2 & 3 but mirrored. Captures the eastern half of Long N from close range.
    90° fan · faces across the table
  • 7
    Long S · 1/3 along
    Continue west along Long S. Face Long N. Sweep right 90°. Covers the western half of Long N.
    90° fan · faces across the table
  • 8
    SW corner
    Final corner. Face along Short A (looking north). Sweep right 90° until you face along Long S. Capture complete.
    90° fan · corner station
Scaling & gotchas
  • 15m room: 2 stations per long wall as shown
  • 20m room: 3 stations per long wall (~5m spacing along each long wall)
  • 25m+ room: 4 stations per long wall + consider splitting into multiple captures
  • Every wall ends up captured from at least 2 stations with translation baseline between them — the SfM-friendly geometry is preserved despite never walking the center
  • If chairs are pulled out into the corridor, walk around them as if they were obstacles — they don't break the protocol, just the visual symmetry
  • Whiteboards on long walls are typically at ~5m from the opposite wall midpoint stations — that's detection-grade pixel density
Continuous Perimeter Walk

For L-shapes, alcoves, multi-corner rooms, or any non-rectangular geometry. No discrete stations — walk the perimeter slowly with the camera pointed at the room interior, pausing briefly at every corner.

Key principle

Concave corners (where walls turn inward) are the failure point of every protocol — they're poorly seen from anywhere outside the alcove. Always pause and pan at a concave corner.

L-shaped room with mixed furniture · perimeter walk around obstacles · pause-and-pan at the concave corner
Pause-and-pan
Concave corner
Walking path
Procedure
  • 1
    Start at any outer corner
    Stand near (but not in) a corner of the room. Hold camera at chest height, pointed at the room interior — roughly toward the opposite far corner.
    camera ~1.5m from walls
  • 2
    Walk slowly along the perimeter
    Stay roughly 1.5–2m from the walls. Walk at a steady pace (~0.5 m/s). Camera stays pointed toward the room interior — not at the wall you're walking past.
    slow, consistent motion
  • 3
    Pause at every convex corner
    When walls turn outward (the typical "corner of a room"), stop for ~1s. Don't pan — just let the camera capture stable frames while your orientation changes.
    ~1s pause
  • 4
    Pan at concave corners
    At any inward-turning corner (the inside of an L, an alcove, a recessed section), stop and pan 90° to capture the inner geometry directly. These corners are poorly seen from anywhere else in the room.
    90° pan · critical for L-shapes
  • 5
    Cover alcoves & recessed areas
    If the room has small recesses (under-stair alcoves, mechanical closets), walk into each one briefly and pan to capture interior surfaces.
    ~5s per alcove
  • 6
    Complete the loop
    Return to the starting point. The capture should loop closed — same orientation at start and end gives the loop-closure constraint that helps SfM correct drift.
    loop closure
Failure modes
  • Concave corners are the #1 failure point — set a deliberate pause-and-pan
  • Walking too fast → motion blur, frames rejected by QA gate
  • Camera pointed AT walls (instead of room interior) → loses parallax baseline; you'd just be panoramic-mapping the wall
  • Skipping alcoves → those surfaces don't exist in the reconstruction
8-Station Hybrid · Corners + Wall Midpoints

For square or near-square boardrooms larger than ~10m on a side. Corner stations alone leave the middle of each wall too far for detail. Add wall-midpoint stations in the corridor between the table and each wall — each one captures the opposite wall from close range.

Key principle

From a 15m × 15m corner, the opposite wall is 21m diagonal away. Adding wall-midpoint stations (in the perimeter corridor, NOT the room center which is blocked by the table) puts every wall within ~10m. The 8 stations together give every surface multi-view coverage with strong baselines.

Notes
  • Every wall ends up captured from 3 stations: 2 corners (at angles, with strong baselines) + 1 wall midpoint (close, perpendicular). That's ideal SfM + segmentation geometry.
  • Rooms over ~20m: insert additional wall stations at ¼ and ¾ along each long wall (12 stations total)
  • If the room has elevated features (mezzanines, hanging displays, high ceilings), tilt camera up at corner stations for an additional pass
  • Watch for perimeter obstacles (credenzas, AV carts, plants) — walk around them, keep camera pointed across the room
  • The order corners → wall-midpoints alternating means you're never walking past a station you've already done; the path is one continuous clockwise loop
